Let’s learn a little bit on how CEG was established.CEG began its journey as a school of survey established by the East India Company in 1794 at the Fort St. George Madras. It was started by Michael Topping an Astronomical Surveyor. The name of the survey school was changed as Civil Engineering School and moved to Kalasa Mahal buildings in Chepauk in the year 1858. The school was then affiliated to Madras University in 1862. In 1920 the College moved into the sprawling 185 acres campus at Guindy. In 1925, Rao Bahadur G. Nagarathinam Ayyar became the first Indian Principal of the College.
